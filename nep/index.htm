Nepal's Turbulent Path to Democracy

A 35-Year Political Saga: 1990 - 2025

From Kingdom to Republic: A Nation in Flux

Since 1990, Nepal has undergone a profound and often volatile transformation, moving from an absolute monarchy to a federal democratic republic. This journey has been marked by popular movements, a decade-long civil war, multiple constitutional rewrites, and persistent political instability. The nation's quest for a stable, inclusive, and accountable governance system continues to evolve, reflecting deep-seated societal tensions and a public yearning for progress and reform.

A Timeline of Constitutional Change

1990: Constitution of the Kingdom of Nepal

Established a constitutional monarchy and multiparty parliamentary system, but retained significant royal powers.

2007: Interim Constitution

Drafted after the civil war, this constitution formally stripped the king of his powers, declared Nepal a secular state, and paved the way for the abolition of the monarchy.

2015: Constitution of Nepal

After years of debate, this constitution was promulgated, establishing Nepal as a federal democratic republic with three tiers of government: federal, provincial, and local.

The Rise of Independent Voices

Widespread disillusionment with established political parties has created an opening for independent candidates. The 2022 Kathmandu mayoral election was a watershed moment, where rapper and engineer Balen Shah secured a landslide victory against candidates from the two largest parties, signaling a major shift in urban voter sentiment.

Balen Shah: Symbol, Not Sovereign

While Balen Shah's victory made him a powerful symbol of change and the hope of a generation, he is unlikely to lead a national revolution in 2025. His influence is a catalyst, but several factors constrain his ability to take national power.

Lack of a National Party

Governing a city as an independent is different from leading a country. Without a party structure, mobilizing support, contesting national elections, and forming a government is nearly impossible.

Focus on Urban Governance

His mandate and experience are in urban management for Kathmandu. The complex issues of national policy, foreign relations, and rural development require a different skill set and political base.

A Movement Larger Than One

The youth-led protests are anti-establishment at their core, not pro-Balen exclusively. He is a figurehead for their frustration, but the movement seeks systemic change, not the coronation of a new leader.
